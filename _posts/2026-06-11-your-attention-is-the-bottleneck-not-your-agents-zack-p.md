## Overview
This video discusses the problem of human attention being the bottleneck in AI development, rather than the capabilities of AI agents themselves. The speaker, Zack Proser, presents a solution to this problem by showcasing a sustainable AI development workflow. This workflow aims to optimize human attention and reduce burnout by leveraging AI agents and automated tools.

## Key Insights
- The proposed stack for sustainable AI development consists of four layers: signal agents, verification gates, weekly agent runs, and holistic well-being integration.
- Signal agents can read Slack and Linear on a loop, eliminating the need for manual checks and reducing context switching.
- Voice-first flows can be used for coding efficiency, with voice briefs at 184 words per minute and remote control via phone or other devices.
- The Shower Principle and Remote Control allow developers to work on tasks while agents handle other tasks in the background.
- Verification gates include lint, build, browser click-through, and critic passes to ensure safety and testing.
- Weekly agent runs can surface inefficiencies and generate missing skills by analyzing JSONL conversation history.

## Tech & Tools
Slack, Linear, Git, JSONL, Oura ring, MCP, Claude, LTE.

## Takeaway
The most important thing to know is that human attention, not agent capabilities, is the primary bottleneck in AI development, and optimizing workflows to reduce burnout and increase efficiency is crucial for sustainable AI development.

## Description

Simon Willison fires up four parallel agents and is wiped out by 11am. That is the problem Zack Proser is solving: not that the tools are too slow but that human attention is still the hard constraint. His loop: voice brief at 184 words per minute, agent dispatched to an isolated git worktree, laptop closed, progress checked from a phone on LTE miles away via remote control.

The talk covers four layers that make this sustainable: signal agents that read Slack and Linear on a loop so you never open them yourself, verification gates from lint and build up to browser click through and critic passes, a weekly agent run over your JSONL conversation history to surface inefficiencies and generate missing skills, and an Oura ring connected via MCP so Claude can tell you that you did not sleep last night. You can ignore it. But at least you thought about it.
